Historical Analysis

The Industrial Dawn

Between 1760 and 1789, humanity unlocked the chemical energy stored in coal. This period marks the transition from muscle and wood power to machine and steam power.

Industrial Activity Index

Cotton Imports & Coal Usage (Proxy Data)

Timeline of Innovation

1760

The Era Begins

Britain begins the transition to new manufacturing processes.

1769

Watt's Patent

James Watt patents the separate condenser. This is the "iPhone moment" for steam power.

1779

The Spinning Mule

Samuel Crompton invents the Spinning Mule, vastly increasing textile output.

1784

Coal & Iron

Henry Cort's puddling process allows coal to be used to refine iron.

Life Before 1760

  • Energy source: Muscles, Animals, Wind, Water.
  • Production: Cottage industries (home-based).
  • Transport: Horse and carriage, sailing ships.
  • CO2 Levels: Stable at ~280ppm.

Life After 1789

  • Energy source: Coal (Fossil Fuels).
  • Production: Large factories & mass production.
  • Transport: Canals, early steam locomotives.
  • CO2 Levels: Beginning the upward trend.

Observed Impacts

Ocean Warming

Oceans absorb 90% of excess heat. This causes coral bleaching, stronger hurricanes, and disrupts marine ecosystems.

Cryosphere Loss

Arctic sea ice is shrinking. Glaciers are retreating worldwide, threatening fresh water supplies for millions.
